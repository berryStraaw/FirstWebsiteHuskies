Breed info

Huskies are a breed of dogs that are used mainly for pulling sleds. They are geneticaly close to an extinct "Taimyr wolf". Originaly they were kept by the local population in the Arctic regions used mainly to transport goods between vilages, they can travel up to 125 miles a day with a speed of 10 to 11 miles per hour speed which is why they are used in the snowy areas where transportations by vehicles is harder to come by

Characteristics

Huskies are extremely energetic and athletic which is the main reason why it is not the best breed for first time dog owners. They show signs of high inteligence, they like to be independent and do their own thing if nobody is there to controll them. They have a double coat which protects them from harsh winters, if they are in a warmer climate they tend to shed their undercoat. Their coat also protects them from the sun as their skin is not designed to handle sunlight. Average weight ranges between 16kg and 27kg, Height 50-60cm

Husky activities

Huskies are usually used in sled pulling competitions, but there are other activities where they can be seen,
Bikejoring is an activity where a dog is running along a person on a bike sometimes even pulling him/her.
Carting is similar to sled pulling expect it is done on dry land with a cart on wheels
Skijoring same as sled pulling with some rules changed, mainly the owners do not have to bring a full backpack with them so it looks more like skiers with dogs pulling them

Similar breeds

There are a few similar breeds to huskies although boths have very different uses, the main two are Malamute and Laika

Malamutes are bigger and flufier huskies, they were used in hunting big animals and pulling heavier sleds than huskies

Laika is a diverse hunting dog which looks similar to huskies, there are different laika breeds such as Yakutian Laika which is used for sled pulling or Nenets Herding Laika used as the name implies, herding.
